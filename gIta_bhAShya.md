**भाष्यम्**  
ॐ देवं नारायणं नत्वा सर्वदोषविवर्जितम् ।  
परिपूर्ण गुरूंश्चाऽन् गीतार्थं वक्ष्यामि लेशतः ।।

Having bowed to the Lord Narayana, who is entirely free from all faults, and to the complete Guru Vyasa, I shall briefly explain the meaning of the Gita.

**प्रमेयदीपिका**  
गुणगणनिलयं पर्ति रमाया जगदघदहनं च वासवीसूनुम् ।  
मुनिकुलतिलकं च पूर्णबोधं गुरुमपि परमं गुरुं च मे वन्दे ॥

I salute that supreme Guru, my Guru, who is the abode of a host of virtues, the consort of Lakshmi, the destroyer of the world's sins, the son of Vasava (Vayu), the crest-jewel of the lineages of sages, and who is full of perfect knowledge.

**आनन्दतीर्थभगवत्पादाचार्यविनिर्मितम् ।**  
**व्याख्यास्ये भगवद्गीताभाष्यं तत्करुणाबलात् ।।**

I will expound the commentary on the Bhagavad Gita, composed by Anandatirtha Bhagavatpada Acharya, by the strength of his compassion.

**प्रारिप्सितविघ्नविघातादिप्रयोजनं समुचितविशिष्टेष्टदेवतागुरुप्रणामं**  
**ग्रन्थारम्भप्रतिज्ञां चाऽदौ निबध्नाति भगवान् भाष्यकारः - देवमिति ।।**

The venerable commentator, at the beginning, incorporates the obeisance to the appropriate, specific, chosen deity and Guru - with the word 'devam' - which serves the purpose of removing obstacles for the intended undertaking, and also the promise to commence the work.

**अत्र**  
**देवशब्दो देवताशब्दपर्यायः प्रतिपाद्यमाह ।**

Here, the word 'deva' is a synonym for the word 'devata' (deity) and states what is to be established.

**सन्निधानाच्च भगवद्गीताया इति**  
**लभ्यते ।**

And from the proximity (of the verse) to the Bhagavad Gita, it is understood.

**तेनेदानीं नारायणस्य प्रणामे समुचिततामाचष्टे ।**

Therefore, it now declares the appropriateness of bowing to Narayana.

**अज्ञान-**  
**पारतन्त्र्यादिभिः सर्वदोषैर्विशेषेण सर्वदा सर्वात्मना वर्जितम् ।**

Who is entirely devoid of all faults, especially those like ignorance and subjection to others, always and in every way.

**परितो**  
**देशतः कालतो गुणतश्च पूर्णमिति वैशिष्टयमाचष्टे ।**

It declares His special characteristic: that He is completely full in every direction, in space, in time, and in qualities.

**परमवैष्णवस्य भगवतो**  
**भाष्यकारस्य विष्णुरिष्टदेवतेति प्रसिद्धमेव ।**

It is well known that for the venerable commentator, who is a supreme Vaishnava, Vishnu is the chosen deity.

**यद्वा, भगवद्गीताप्रतिपाद्यत्वं**  
**नारायणस्य न परेषामिव जीवत्वनिर्विशेषत्वादिरूपेण, किं नाम ?**

Or else, the meaning is: The state of being the subject matter of the Bhagavad Gita, for Narayana, is not like for others, in the form of being a non-distinct jiva, etc. Then what is it?

**सर्वदोष-**  
**वर्जिततया पूर्णतया चेति पदद्वयेनाऽख्याति ।**

It is stated by the two words ('sarvadosha-varjitam' and 'paripurnam') as being free from all faults and as being complete.

**आन् व्यासान् ।**

'An' means (the sage) Vyasa.

**'अ आ अं**  
**अः पुराणर्षिः व्यासः शाखासहस्रकृत्' इत्यकारस्य तन्नामसु पाठात् ।**

Because the letter 'a' is listed among His names (in the Vishnu Sahasranama): 'A, Aa, Am, Ah; the ancient sage Vyasa, the creator of a thousand branches (of the Veda).'

**बहुवचनं**  
**च गौरवादेव ।**

And the plural form is used only to show respect.

**गीतेति भगवद्गीतैवाभिप्रेयते ।**

The word 'gita' refers specifically to the Bhagavad Gita.

**'मुख्यामुख्ययोः मुख्ये कार्य-**  
**सम्प्रत्ययः' इति न्यायात् ।**

By the maxim: "When there is a primary and a secondary (meaning), the presumption of the purpose (of the statement) lies in the primary."

**यद्यपि बह्वर्था गीता, शक्तश्चाहं तद्वचने, तथाऽपि**  
**श्रोतॄणां मन्दां मतिमपेक्ष्याल्पमेवोच्यत इति भावेनोक्तम्- लेशत इति ॥**

Although the Gita has many meanings and I am capable of stating them, still, it is said with the sentiment that, considering the dull intellect of the listeners, only a little is being spoken - hence the word 'lesatah' (briefly).

**इत्थम्भूतलक्षणार्थतृतीयान्तात् तसिः ।**

(The suffix 'tas' in 'lesatah') is the 'tas' suffix (meaning 'from' or 'in what manner') from a third case ending which has the sense of a characteristic of such a kind.

**अर्थशब्देन सर्वार्थग्रहणप्रसक्तौ सर्वेषां**  
**च भाष्येऽदर्शनात् वन्ध्येयं प्रतिज्ञेति मन्दाशङ्कानिरासायेदमुदितम् ।**

This is stated to dispel the faint doubt that, since the word 'artha' (meaning) could imply the grasping of *all* meanings, and since all (meanings) are not seen in the commentary, this promise is barren (unfulfilled).

**नन्वस्तु नमस्कारग्रथनं शिष्यशिक्षार्थम् ।**  
**प्रतिज्ञा तु किमर्थं ग्रन्थे**  
**निवेशनीया ?**

Now, let it be that the inclusion of the salutation is for the instruction of the student. But for what reason is the promise to be included in the text?

**उच्यते ।**  
**बहुभिर्व्याख्यातृभिः भगवद्गीताया व्याख्यातत्वाद्**  
**व्यर्थमिदं भाष्यमित्याशङ्कानिरसनं प्रयोजनमस्य ।**

It is replied: The purpose of this (promise) is the removal of the doubt that, since the Bhagavad Gita has been commented upon by many commentators, this commentary is useless.

**अन्यैः स्वपक्षनिक्षिप्त-**  
**दृष्टिभिः स्वाभिप्रायो भगवद्गीतार्थत्वेनाऽरोप्योक्तः ।**

By others, with their vision fixed only on their own school of thought, their own opinion has been stated by superimposing it as the meaning of the Bhagavad Gita.

**अहं तु गीतार्थमेव**  
**वक्ष्यामीति सूचनात् ।**

But I will state only the meaning of the Gita itself - this is the indication.

**नचात्र यतिभङ्गो दोषः ।**  
**तस्य काव्यविषयत्वात् ।**

And here there is no fault of a break in the metrical pause (yati-bhanga), because that (rule) pertains to the realm of poetry (kavya).

**शास्त्रे तु 'य उपाधिर्लगति साध्ये सम्बन्धविशेषणे' इत्याद्यभियुक्तवाक्ये**  
**दर्शनादिति ।**

Whereas in scriptural texts (shastra), it is seen in the statements of the learned, such as "ya upadhir lagati..." etc.

**भाष्यम्**
**नष्टधर्मज्ञानलोककृपालुभिः ब्रह्मरुद्रेन्द्रादिभिरर्थितो ज्ञानप्रदर्शनाय भगवान् व्यासोऽवततार ।**

Requested by (deities like) Brahmā, Rudra, and Indra—who are compassionate towards the worlds where धर्म (righteousness) and ज्ञान (knowledge) were lost—the Lord, Vyāsa, descended to demonstrate (that) knowledge.

**प्रमेयदीपिका**
**ननुच प्राधान्येन व्याख्येयता प्रामाण्ये, अधिकारिविषयप्रयोजनाभिसम्बन्धे, दुर्गमार्थत्वे च सति सम्भवति ।**

Now, (the characteristic of) being a primary subject for commentary arises when (a text possesses) authority, a connection with a qualified student, a subject matter, and a purpose, and when its meaning is difficult to grasp.

**तत् कथं भगवद्गीतायाः प्रामाण्यादिकम् ?**

Therefore, how (can one establish) the authority and other (attributes) of the Bhagavadgītā?

**सति वा तस्मिन् तथाविधग्रन्थान्तरपरिहारेणास्या एव व्याख्याने को हेतुरित्याशङ्कय महाभारतान्तर्गतत्वाद् भगवद्गीतायाः, तस्य प्रामाण्यादिसिद्धौ तस्या अपि तत् सेत्स्यतीत्याशयवान् महाभारतस्य प्रामाण्यादिकं साधयितुमाह- नष्टेति ।।**

Or, if that (authority) exists, what is the reason for commenting on this (Gītā) specifically, to the exclusion of other similar texts? Having anticipated this doubt, (the commentator) intends that since the Bhagavadgītā is within the Mahābhārata, when the authority, etc., of the Mahābhārata is established, that of the Gītā will also be established. Therefore, he begins to establish the authority, etc., of the Mahābhārata with (the word) 'naṣṭa...' etc.

**किमनया कुटिलिकया ?**

Why this indirect approach?

**गीताया एव पृथक् प्रामाण्यादिकं साध्यताम् ।**

Let the authority, etc., of the Gītā alone be established separately.

**मैवम् ।**

This should not be so.

**महावाक्यप्रामाण्याद्यनभिधाने अवान्तरवाक्यप्रामाण्यादेरसम्भावनाग्रस्तत्वेन साधयितुमशक्यत्वात् ।**

Because if the authority, etc., of the great text (Mahābhārata) is not stated, then the authority, etc., of its subsidiary statements (like the Gītā) would be considered impossible (to establish) and thus cannot be established.

**यथोक्तम् - 'सम्भावितः प्रतिज्ञाया अर्थस्साध्येत हेतुना' इति ।**

As it is said: "The meaning of a proposition can be established by a reason (only) if it is plausible."

**नष्टशब्दोऽदर्शनवाची।**

The word 'naṣṭa' means 'unseen' or 'lost'.

**'णश अदर्शने' इति स्मरणात् ।**

This is remembered from (the dhātu) 'ṇaśa' (meaning) 'to disappear'.

**धर्मो निवृत्तिलक्षणः ।**

धर्म here is characterized as cessation (from unrighteousness) or the path to liberation.

**ज्ञानशब्देन ज्ञेयं परतत्त्वं वासुदेवाख्यमपरं च प्रकृत्यादिकं लक्ष्यते ।**

By the word 'ज्ञान', the object of knowledge is indicated, namely the Supreme Reality called Vāsudeva, and the other (reality), namely Primordial Matter (प्रकृति) and so forth.

**लक्षणायाश्च प्रयोजनं, यथा धर्मो ज्ञात्वा उत्पादनीयः न तथेदम्, किन्तु ज्ञानपरम्परैव तद्विषयाऽनुष्ठेयेति ज्ञापनम् ।**

And the purpose of this indication is as follows: It is to make known that unlike धर्म, which, once known, is to be practiced (or produced), this (ज्ञान) is not so; rather, only a continuous stream of knowledge is to be practiced regarding its object.

**लोक इति सज्जनो विवक्षितः ।**

The word 'loka' (world) here intends (to mean) the good people.

**नष्टेऽदर्शनं गते धर्मज्ञाने यस्य तस्मिन् लोके विषये कृपालुभिः ।**

(It means) by those who are compassionate towards that 'loka' (the good people) for whom धर्म and ज्ञान have become lost, i.e., have disappeared.

**यद्यपि 'स्पृहिगृहि' इत्यादिना कृपेरालुच्प्रत्ययो न विहितः । तथाऽपि व्याकरणान्तरबलादवगन्तव्यः ।**

Although the suffix 'āluč' is not prescribed for the root 'kṛp' by (rules like those beginning with) 'spṛhi-gṛhi' etc., nevertheless, it must be understood based on the authority of other grammatical systems.

**कलशवाची वाऽऽलुशब्दः । कृपापूर्णकलशैरित्यर्थः ।**

Or, the word 'ālu' means a water pot. The meaning is 'those who are like pots full of compassion'.

**मोक्षसाधनयोः धर्मज्ञानयोरदर्शनात् संसारे क्लिश्यमानान् सज्जनानवलोक्य उत्पन्नकपैरिति यावत् ।**

That is to say, (they are) those in whom compassion arose upon seeing the good people suffering in the world due to the disappearance of धर्म and ज्ञान, which are the means to liberation.

**अन्यपदार्थानां देवानां ब्रह्मरुद्रेन्द्रादिभिरिति यद् विशेषणं तत् प्रसिद्धप्रभावानामपि भगवदितरेषामशक्यमिदं कार्यमिति ज्ञापनार्थम् ।**

The description 'by Brahmā, Rudra, Indra, etc.', who are other deities (literally, whose meaning/purpose is other), is to indicate that this task is impossible even for those other than the Lord, despite their famous powers.

**ज्ञानपदेन धर्मोऽप्युपलक्ष्यते ।**

By the word 'ज्ञान', धर्म is also implied.

**समकक्ष्यत्वनिरासाय स्वशब्देनाग्रहणम् ।**

(The word) 'ज्ञान' is used (and not 'dharma') by its own specific term to avoid the notion that they are of equal status (or to specifically exclude dharma from this context).

**एतच्च पूर्वेणोत्तरेण च सम्बद्धयते- ज्ञानप्रदर्शनायार्थितोज्ञानप्रदर्शनायावततारेति ।**

And this (phrase) is connected with both what precedes and what follows: 'Requested for the demonstration of knowledge... descended for the demonstration of knowledge.'

**भगवान् समग्रज्ञानैश्वर्यवैराग्यादिगुणसम्पन्नो नारायणो व्यासरूपेणावततारेत्यर्थः ।**

The meaning is: The Lord, Nārāyaṇa, endowed with qualities such as complete knowledge, sovereignty, detachment, etc., descended in the form of Vyāsa.
