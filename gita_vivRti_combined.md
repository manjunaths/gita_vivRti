When protection is done through dharma, who upheld dharma and who upheld adharma?—this is the implied meaning.Again, in the spiritual sense—'Māmakāḥ' (mine)—'maḥ' in Śiva and 'mā' in Rāmā, thus from the universe; 'maḥ śivaḥ' (Śiva is mine), knowing Him as the Self, hence 'māmakaḥ'—Śaṅkara, because of the declaration 'Śivo'ham' (I am Śiva); his disciples are 'māmakāḥ'—meaning the Māyāvādins.The suffix 'ka' indicates contempt.'Pāṇḍavāḥ'—Pāṇḍu means 'white-complexioned' like the wind, as stated in 'udyadraviprakarasannibha' (resembling the rising sun), due to his knowledge-oriented nature; from the root 'paṇḍ' meaning 'to go,' and since verbs of motion imply knowledge, and due to the statement 'tācchīlyārthādunastathe' (indicating habitual action), his disciples are the Pāṇḍavas—meaning Vaiṣṇavas.'Dharma-kṣetra'—'Dharma is known by the best knower of dharma,' thus dharma is Viṣṇu; 'kṣetra'—that by which one is known or dwells, from the root 'kṣi' meaning 'to dwell' or 'to move,' and since verbs of motion imply knowledge, with the suffix '-tṛn,' meaning it expounds Viṣṇu.'Kuru-kṣetra'—'a-ku' means 'flawless,' from the root 'ruś' (to purify); 'śabda-kṣetra'—the abode of words, meaning the Vedas, as stated in 'nirdoṣaḥ śabdabhāgama' (flawless portion of sound), implying the Vedas solely expound Bhagavān.In qualifying the Pāṇḍavas, the word 'asamavetāḥ' means 'not equal'—unequal in which it exists, thus 'asama' (graded), meaning they are united with such knowledge.Or, 'There is none equal to Nārāyaṇa,' thus 'asamaḥ'—Viṣṇu, His knowledge, and thus they are united with it.Or, 'In equality, sameness is proper,' thus 'saṃ-abheda' (non-difference), its opposite being 'asambheda' (difference); knowing that distinction—those who have realized it, meaning they are knowers of difference.In the word 'samavetāḥ,' 'sama'—the flawless Brahman, as stated in 'nirdoṣaṃ hi samaṃ brahma' (Brahman is flawless and equal), or the knowledge of Lakṣmī's consort, and thus united with it.

The words are parsed as: "In Dharmakṣetra (Kurukṣetra), O A-yuyutsavaḥ (those averse to war), may my sons (āmakāḥ) and the Pāṇḍavas—what did they do, O Sañjaya?"Dharmakṣetra" is twofold: Dharma (actions pleasing to Viṣṇu) and Kṣetra (knowledge, from the root "kṣi" meaning "to abide/dwell," as motion implies knowledge). What did they do? O Sañjaya, "āmakāḥ" means "unripe (āmāḥ) in the womb"—Duryodhana and others (selfish ones). The rhetorical "kim" implies they did nothing (blameless). The Pāṇḍavas alone ("eva") in Kurukṣetra performed both righteous action (kuru-karma) and knowledge (kṣetra-jñāna) perfectly—hence, O Sañjaya, "lead them to complete victory" (implied). Here, "ayuyutsa" is a separate word addressing Kṛṣṇa ("O Unwilling-to-fight"). It means "one who has no desire for war" (yuyutsā = battle-lust). When addressing Sañjaya, "ayuyutsuvaḥ" is one word meaning "Vāsudeva" (by partial naming convention). Alternatively, "may He (Kṛṣṇa, the embodiment of knowledge) protect me (mā avet)"—using the archaic middle voice (ātmanepada). Or, "O A-yuyutsavaḥ (Vyāsa, Kṛṣṇa, Sañjaya—those averse to war), may you all protect me" (second-person plural optative). The active voice (parasmaipada) is used to emphasize their altruism—implied: "from the sin of associating with wicked sons." In the spiritual sense:

रा०वि० अत्र सेनायां महेष्वासाः-महाधनुष्का: युधि भीमार्जुनसमाः शूराः सन्ति । ते के इत्यत आह । युयुधानइत्यादिना । महारथइति प्रत्येकमन्वेति ॥ ४ ॥त्रि०वि० ॥ तेषां विशेषान्तरमाह ॥ अत्रेति ॥ अध्यात्मार्थे अत्र - अस्यां चम्वां युधि- सम्प्रहारविषये शूराः - विक्रमिणः, भीमार्जुनसमाः भृताः - पूर्णाः,मा:- ज्ञानोपलक्षितबलादीनि यस्य स भीम: "भृतमोभीम" इत्युक्तेः सवायुरित्यर्थः "वायुर्भीम" इत्युक्तेः,अर्ज्यते पारमेष्टयं पदमनेनेति अर्जुनो-ब्रह्मा, "अर्जअर्जने" "अर्जे अर्जिणि लुक्वेत्यनन्" "अल्पाच्तरं पूर्व" मिति सूत्रात्,भीमशब्दस्यादौ निपातः तावुभौ, समौ-महदभिमानित्वेन समानयोग्यतावन्तौ येषु ते ।महेष्वासाः इषुः-ज्ञानं "इषगतिहिंसादर्शनेषु" गत्यर्थानां च ज्ञानार्थत्वात्, "इषः किचेत्युः" तस्मिन् आसा-निष्ठा, इष्वासा, महती-पूर्णा महेष्वासा येषान्ते, सन्तीति शेषः ।विक्रामणो देवानेवाह ।युयुधानइति "युयुवियोग" इति धातोः, विविक्तधानाकारचिन्हवान् शेषः, गोधूमाकृतिचिन्हानां नागे प्रसिद्धेः ।विराटः-विशिष्टं-सर्वोत्कृष्टं, रमणं-सुखं यस्य तं विष्णुं अटयति-गमयति वाहयतीतियावत्, सगरुडइत्यर्थः ।द्रुपदो-रुद्रः, उभयसमुच्चये च शब्दौ, एते त्रयः अहंकारतत्वाभिमानिनः ।
Here (in this army) are mighty warriors, great archers equal to Bhima and Arjuna in battle—Yuyudhana (Satyaki), Virata, and Drupada, the great charioteer.Bhima" (भीम) signifies one whose strength and other qualities are marked by knowledge ("भृतमोभीम"), meaning "filled with the wind" (वायुर्भीम).Arjuna" (अर्जुन) means one who attains the highest state (ब्रह्मा), derived from the root "अर्ज" (to earn), with the suffix "अनन्" (as per grammar rules). In this army, there are great archers (महेष्वासाः) who are equal to Bhima and Arjuna in battle. Who are they? The verse names them: Yuyudhana (Satyaki), Virata, and Drupada, each a महारथ (great chariot-warrior). In the spiritual interpretation (अध्यात्मार्थे), "here" refers to this army. In the context of battle (युधि), the "heroes" (शूराः) are those endowed with valor, equal to Bhima and Arjuna—filled with their qualities. The term "Bhima" is a prefix, and both (Bhima and Arjuna) are equal in greatness and capability. The great archers (महेष्वासाः) are those for whom the bow (इषुः) is knowledge, because words denoting motion (like 'to go') are used in the sense of knowledge; the act of discharging (आसा) is fixed in that (arrow); they are 'इष्वासा' (archers); those whose great (महती) discharging (आसा) is full (पूर्णा) are 'महेष्वासा' (great archers); the rest (of the compound) means 'they are'. The name 'विक्रान्त' (Vikrānta) refers to the devas (gods) themselves. 'Yuyudhāna' is from the root 'yuyuviyoga' (to separate); the remainder (of the compound) means 'possessing a distinct mark of grain'; this is well-known for serpents (nāgas) which have marks shaped like wheat grains. 'Virāṭa' means that which is distinguished, most excellent; it causes Viṣṇu, whose delight (ramanam) is happiness (sukham), to move, to go, to convey—i.e., he is with Garuḍa. 'Drupada' is Rudra; the words (in the compound) are in a dual conjunction; these three are those who identify with the principle of ego (ahaṅkāra-tattva).

अतएव महारथा 'इत्युक्तं, महत्-महत्तत्वं, रथः-कारणत्वेन गतिर्यस्य सोऽहंकारः "रथगता"विति धातोः ।अभिमान्यभिमन्यमानयोरैक्यव्यपदेशादिति भावः ।एकवचनं तु पार्थक्येन योज्यं ॥ ॥ अंशार्थे ॥भीमार्जुनौ-वाखिन्द्रौ "भीमसेनः पुरा वायु"रित्युक्तेः, "मर्जुनः पूर्वमेवेन्द्र" इति चोक्तेः, समत्वकथनं तु अन्यथाज्ञानित्वात् ।युयुधानः-कृष्णपक्षाभिमानी देवः "कृष्णपक्षो युयुधानाभिधेय" इत्युक्तेः,विराटः हाहानामा ब्रह्मगायको गन्धर्वः, "विराटस्तु हाहानामा गायको ब्रह्मणः पुरा" इत्युक्तेः ।द्रुपदो-हूहूगन्धर्वः "द्रुपदस्तु पुरा प्रोक्तः स हूहूर्नाम गायक" इत्युक्तेः ॥ ॥ प्रसिद्धार्थ ॥युयुधानः सात्यकिः, तेषामेव महारथत्वकथनं तु स्वपक्षीयवचोब्रुवाणस्य रामस्य कृष्णसमक्षमपि सात्यकिना निराकरणात् ।विराटोत्तरगोग्रहणे पराजितत्वात् ।गुरुदक्षिणादानकाले द्रुपदेनापि पराजितत्वादिति बोध्यं ॥ ४ ॥
Therefore, it is said 'mahārathāḥ' (great charioteers); 'mahāt' is the great principle (mahat-tattva); 'rathaḥ' is that whose movement is due to its causal nature—that is the ego (ahaṅkāra), from the root 'rathagatā' (to go by chariot). The idea is that there is a declaration of identity between the one who possesses ego (abhimānī) and the one who is identified with it (abhimanyamāna). The singular number, however, should be applied separately. (This is) in the sense of (them being) portions (of the divine). Bhīma and Arjuna are (portions of) Vāyu and Indra, as it is said "Bhīmasena was previously Vāyu" and "Arjuna was formerly Indra"; the statement of their equality, however, is due to (the speaker) knowing otherwise (i.e., not knowing their true divine nature). Yuyudhāna is a deva (deity) who identifies with the party of Kṛṣṇa, as it is said "The one named Yuyudhāna is (of) Kṛṣṇa's party". Virāṭa is a Gandharva (celestial musician) named Hāhā, who sings for Brahmā, as it is said "Virāṭa, indeed, was previously the singer named Hāhā for Brahmā". Drupada is the Gandharva named Hūhū, as it is said "Drupada, indeed, was previously declared to be the singer named Hūhū". (This is) the well-known meaning. Yuyudhāna is Sātyaki; the statement of their being great charioteers (mahārathas) is because even when Rāma (Balarāma), who was speaking for his own side, (said it) in the very presence of Kṛṣṇa, it was refuted by Sātyaki. (Because) Virāṭa was defeated during the capture of the Uttara's cattle. It should be understood that Drupada was also defeated at the time of giving the Guru-dakṣiṇā (preceptor's fee). The state of being a 'Mahāratha' is defined as: "He who alone can fight against ten thousand archers and is skilled in weapons and scriptures is remembered as a Mahāratha." Vikrāntaḥ - the courageous one. Saubhadraḥ - Abhimanyu. Draupadeyāḥ - the sons of Draupadī. (End of verse 6) (Tri. Vi.) He now describes the others, starting with Dhṛṣṭaketu. Cekitānaḥ - Those that glitter are 'Cekinaḥ' (from the root 'cakadīptau' [to glitter], with a guṇa change due to the 'pṛṣodarādi' rule), meaning peacock feathers; he who has their expansion (tānaḥ) is 'Cekitānaḥ' - a peacock; since it is his vehicle, by the rule 'arśa-ādyac' (a suffix meaning 'whose vehicle is'), it means Skanda - as it is said "Viśākha (Skanda) has a peacock (as his vehicle)"; the idea is that these two (Cekitāna and another) identify with the principle of mind (manas-tattva). Kāśīrājaḥ - That which shines (kāśyante) everywhere is 'Kāśyaḥ' - the directions; due to their pervasiveness, their lord (rājaḥ) is the lord of the directions (dikdevāḥ); this means these (two) identify with the sense of hearing (śrotra).

पुरु-पूर्णतमः जयतीति पुरुजित् सूर्यः चक्षुरभिमानीत्यर्थः ।कुंतिभोजः-कुं-भूमिगुणं-गन्धं तनोति-विस्तारयतीति कुन्ति-अन्नादि, तद्भोजयति तद्रसंज्ञापयतीति यावत्, सवरुणः-जिव्हामिमानीत्यर्थः ।शैब्यः-शवान्त-गच्छन्ति-जानन्ति गन्धमनेनेति शिवि-घ्राणेन्द्रियं, 'बाहुलकादित्वं वबयोरभेदश्च' तदभिमानित्वाच्चैव्यः-अश्विनौ, जात्येकवचनम् घ्राणाभिमानिनावित्यर्थः ।चनरपुंगवः, एतद्युधामन्योर्विशेषणम्, च-परं नर-पुंगवोयस्येति ।युधा-सम्प्रहारेण सहिताः मन्यवः-यागाःयस्मात् आग्नेयास्त्रेण युद्धसाधनत्वात्, हुताशनत्वेन यज्ञसाधनत्वाच्च, युधामन्युः-अग्निः-वागभिमानीत्यर्थः ।नरपुंगवः जीवश्रेष्टः, तस्यैव दक्षत्वात् पाण्यभिरामित्वम् ।विक्रान्तः-विक्रमवान्, तस्यैव जयविस्तारकत्वाज्जयन्तः-पादाभिमानी ।उत्तमौजाः महासामर्थ्यवान्, तस्यैव मानत्राणकर्तृत्वान्मित्रः-पाय्वाभिमानी ।वीर्यवान्-वीर्यं-रेतः अधीनत्वेन अस्यास्तीति सतथा मनुः, अतएवोपस्थाभिमानी ।सौभद्रः-शोभनानि भद्राणि-मंगलानि यस्याः सकाशात्सर्वेषामिति, सर्वमंगलत्वात् सुभद्रा-पार्वती, तत्पुत्रःसौभद्रः-गणपतिः-आकाशाभिमानी-तदुपलक्षितमरीचिकादयः, वाय्वाद्यभिमानिनइत्यपि ग्राह्यं ॥
Vīryavān - From the implied meaning (within the word), from the root 'vīgatā' (to go), and because words denoting motion are used in the sense of knowledge; 'vīḥ' is that which indicates touch (sparśa); 'yavān' is the vital air (prāṇa) that identifies with the barley seed (yava-bīja); this means he identifies with the skin (tvak, the sense organ for touch). Puru- the most complete (pūrṇatamaḥ); he who conquers (jayati) is 'Purujit' - the sun; this means he identifies with the eye (cakṣus, the sense of sight). Kuntibhojaḥ - 'Kum' is the earth's quality - smell (gandha); that which spreads (tanoti) it is 'Kunti' - food, etc.; he who makes one consume (bhojayati) it, or rather, makes one perceive its taste (rasa); he is Varuṇa; this means he identifies with the tongue (jihvā, the sense of taste). Śaibyaḥ - 'Śava' means to go, to know; that by which one goes (knows) smell (gandha) is 'Śivi' - the olfactory sense (ghrāṇendriyaṃ); due to the 'bāhulaka' rule and the non-difference of 'ś' and 'ch', and because they identify with it, 'Caivyaḥ' are the Aśvins; the singular number is used to denote the class; this means they identify with the sense of smell (ghrāṇa). 'Ca narapuṁgavaḥ' - This is an adjective for Yudhāmanyu; 'ca' is connected (to mean) 'he whose (superior) is a bull among men (nara-puṁgavaḥ)'. Yudhā - with combat (samprahāreṇa); 'manyavaḥ' are sacrifices (yāgāḥ) because, due to the Āgneyāstra (fire weapon) being a means for battle, and due to fire (hutāśana) being a means for sacrifice; Yudhāmanyuḥ is Agni (Fire); this means he identifies with speech (vāk). Narapuṁgavaḥ - the best of beings (jīva-śreṣṭhaḥ); because of his dexterity (dakṣatvāt), he is delightful to the hand (pāṇy-abhirāmitvam - i.e., skilled in handling). Vikrāntaḥ - possessing prowess (vikramavān); because he himself is the spreader of victory, he is Jayanta - meaning he identifies with the feet (pāda). Uttamaujāḥ - possessing great power (mahā-sāmarthyavān); because he himself is the protector of honor (māna-trāṇa-kartṛtvāt), he is Mitra - meaning he identifies with the anus (pāyu). Vīryavān - 'Vīrya' is semen (retaḥ); he for whom it exists in a controlled state (adhīnatvena) is thus Manu; therefore, he identifies with the genitals (upastha). Saubhadraḥ - 'Subhadrā' is she from whose presence (sakāśāt) come all (sarveṣām) auspicious (śobhanāni) and blessed (bhadrāṇi - maṅgalāni) things; because she is all-auspicious, she is Subhadrā - Pārvatī; her son (tat-putraḥ) is Saubhadraḥ - Gaṇapati; he identifies with space (ākāśa) - and by this, the rays of light (marīcikādayaḥ) are also indicated; it should also be understood that he identifies with Vāyu (Wind) and others.

ननु सर्वेषां पार्थक्येन वचनादस्य वाय्वादिचतुष्टयस्य देवानामेवोपलक्ष्यत्वे किं निमित्तमिति न शंक्यं ।त्वग्वाग्जिव्हादैवतत्वेन वायुतेजोब्देवानां प्राणाग्निवरुणानामुक्तत्वात्पृथिव्याश्च तन्नामदेवताकत्वादिति ध्येयम् ॥द्रौपदेयाः द्रुपदः-शिवइति पूर्वमुक्तः, तत्पुत्र द्रौपदेयाः-प्राणाषा नव्यानोदानसमानाः-पंच शब्दादिविषयाभिमानिनः ।शब्दाद्याः पंच शिवजाः शब्दनात्स्पर्शनादपीत्यादिसर्वे ब्रह्मवाय्वादयो महारथाः-महान्तःअराः-दोषाः यस्मिंस्तस्मिन्महारं शरीरं तस्मिन्नधिकृत्य,थः-स्थितिर्येषान्ते तथाविधाएवतथौ प्राणात्मकौ प्रोक्तौ व्याप्तिस्थितिविधायकौब्रह्मा तु पुरुषः स्मृतः ।महांश्चाव्यक्तनाम्नी तु ब्रह्माणी सम्प्रकीर्तिता ॥एवं वायुरपि ज्ञेयो भारती चैव तत्रयम् ।
Now, it should not be doubted what the reason is for this fourfold group (Vāyu, etc.) of devas being indicated by the separate mention of all (the senses). Because the deities of the skin, speech, and tongue are Vāyu (Wind), Agni (Fire), and Varuṇa (Water), and because the earth (pṛthivī) also has that (name) as its deity, this is to be meditated upon. Draupadeyāḥ - Drupada was stated before to be Śiva; his sons (tat-putrāḥ) are the Draupadeyāḥ - the five vital airs: Prāṇa, Apāna, Vyāna, Udāna, Samāna; they identify with the objects of the senses, such as sound (śabda-ādi-viṣaya). The five (elements) beginning with sound are born from Śiva; from sound (comes) touch, and so on. All, beginning with Brahmā and Vāyu, are great charioteers (or great beings). (They are) spokes (or defects); in that great body, with regard to it, they whose existence is of that very nature. Those two (Brahmā and Vāyu) are declared to be of the nature of the vital air, the bestowers of pervasion and stability. Indeed, Brahmā is remembered as the Puruṣa (the male principle). And the great unmanifest one is proclaimed as Brahmāṇī.

रुद्रोऽहंकारउद्दिष्टः स्कन्देन्द्रौ मनउच्यते ।अहंकारः शेषवीन्द्रावपि विद्वद्भिरीरितौ ।दिग्वायुसूनुसूर्याश्च वरुणश्चाश्विनावपि ।वन्हिदक्षाविन्द्र-सूनुर्मित्रश्चैव मनुस्तथा ।इन्द्रियाख्याः शब्दनामा बृहस्पतिरुदाहृतः ।अन्ये तु सूनवो वायोरुद्रस्यापि प्रकीर्तिता" इति तन्त्रसारे ॥अंशार्थे-धृष्टकेतुः संवर्ती मरुत्धृष्टकेतुः शैशुपालिः संवर्तोमरुदुच्यते" इत्युक्तेः ।चेकितानः प्रतिभाख्योमरुत्चेकितानः पुरा प्रोक्तः प्रतिभोनाम वै मरुत्" इत्युक्तेः ।
Similarly, Vāyu is to be known, and also Bhāratī (Sarasvatī) as his consort. Rudra is indicated as Ahaṅkāra (ego); Skanda and Indra are said to be Manas (mind). Ahaṅkāra (ego), Śeṣa (the serpent) and Indra are also declared by the wise. The quarters (of space), Vāyu, the son (Hanumān?), Sūrya, and Varuṇa, and also the Aśvins. Fire, Dakṣa, the son of Indra (Arjuna?), Mitra, and also Manu. The senses are named; Bṛhaspati is mentioned. Others, however, the sons of Vāyu and also of Rudra are proclaimed" in the Tantrasāra. In the sense of (being an) part/incarnation: Dhṛṣṭaketu is Saṃvarta Marut. Because it is said: "Dhṛṣṭaketu is Śiśupāla; Saṃvarta is called a Marut." Cekitāna is the Marut named Pratibha. Because it is said: "Cekitāna was formerly declared to be a Marut named Pratibha."

काशीराजः कश्चिद्देवः देवांशपक्षसाहाय्यकारित्वात्ये पाण्डवानामभवन्सहाया देवाश्च देवानुचराः समस्ताः" इत्युक्तेः ।पुरुजिदिति विशेषणं ।कुन्तिभोजः कूर्माख्यः प्राणःभोजराजः कूर्मकारव्यमरुदंशयुतः सदे"त्युक्तेः ।शैब्यः ऋभ्वाख्योदेवःशैब्यस्तु ऋभुदेवते" त्युक्तेः ।अस्माकं तु विशिष्टा ये तान्निबोध दिजोत्तम ।नायका मम सैन्यस्य संज्ञार्थ तान्बवीमि ते ॥७॥युधामन्युः-विश्वावसुगन्धर्वःयुधामन्युःपार्षतस्य ब्रह्मावेशयुतोबली । सुतोविश्वावसुर्नामागन्धर्वः समुदाहृत" इत्युक्तेः ।
The king of Kāśī is a certain god, due to rendering help to the side of the partial incarnations of the gods. Because it is said: "All those who became the helpers of the Pāṇḍavas were gods and the attendants of the gods." 'Purujit' is an adjective. Kuntibhoja is the vital air called Kūrma (the tortoise). Because it is said: "King Bhoja is always endowed with the part of the Marut who is the agent of Kūrma." Śaibya is the god named Ṛbhū. Because it is said: "Śaibya, indeed, is the deity Ṛbhu." But, O best of the twice-born, know those who are the distinguished ones on our side. I am telling you the leaders of my army for the purpose of identification. Yudhāmanyu is the Gandharva Viśvāvasu.

उत्तमौजाः-परावसुगन्धर्वःउत्तमौजा यमावेशयुक्तश्चैव परावसु"रित्युक्तेः ।सौभद्रः बुधःअभिमन्यु-बुधः पूर्व" मित्युक्तेः ।द्रौपदेयाः-विश्वेदेवाःपंच पार्थसुताः प्रोक्ता विश्वेदेवाः पुरातना" इत्युक्तेः ।महारथाः-पूज्यरूपाःमह-पूजायां" "शरीरं रथमेवचे"ति काठकश्रुतेः ॥प्रसिद्धार्थ ॥धृष्टकेतुः-शिशुपालसुतः, धर्मजशालकत्वादंतरंगः ।
Because it is said: "The powerful son of Pārṣata (Dṛṣṭadyumna), Yudhāmanyu, endowed with the power of Brahmā, is declared to be the Gandharva named Viśvāvasu." Uttamaujā is the Gandharva Parāvasu. Because it is said: "Uttamaujā, endowed with the power of Yama, is (the Gandharva) Parāvasu." Saubhadra (Abhimanyu) is the planet Mercury (Budha). Because it is said: "Previously, Abhimanyu (is) Budha." The sons of Draupadī are the Viśvedevās. Because it is said: "The five sons of Pārtha (the Pāṇḍavas) are proclaimed as the ancient Viśvedevās." Mahārathas are of a venerable nature. According to the Kāṭhaka Śruti: "`Maha' (means) in worship" and "The body is indeed the chariot." (This is) the well-known meaning. Dhṛṣṭaketu is the son of Śiśupāla; because he is an excellent follower of dharma, he is intimate (to the Pāṇḍavas).

चेकितानः-यादवः कृष्णसेवकत्वात्,काशीराजः भीमश्वशुरत्वात्,कुन्तिभोजः-कुन्तीपितृत्वात्,शैब्यः-श्रीकृष्णभक्तत्वात्,युधामन्युः-पार्षतवंश्यत्वात,उत्तमौजाः-तद्वंश्यत्वात्,सौभद्रः-अभिमन्युः-अर्जुनपुत्रत्वात्,द्रौपदेयाः-द्रौपदीसुतत्वात्, अन्तरंगाएव ।न केवलमन्तरंगाः किंन्तु महारथाअपीति परोत्कर्षासहिष्णुत्वेन वर्णनं बोध्यम् ॥ ५ ॥ ६ ॥रा० वि० अस्माकान्त्वति पाण्डवव्यावर्तकस्तु शब्दः ।अस्माकं सम्बन्धिनो विशिष्टाः-बलिष्ठाः-प्रम सैन्यस्य नायकाः नेतारो ये तान्निबोध-बुध्यस्य जानीहि ।
Cekitāna is a Yādava; because he is a servant of Kṛṣṇa, The king of Kāśī (is intimate) because he is the father-in-law of Bhīma, Kuntībhoja (is intimate) because he is the father of Kuntī, Śaibya (is intimate) because he is a devotee of Śrī Kṛṣṇa, Yudhāmanyu (is intimate) because he belongs to the family of Pārṣata (Dṛṣṭadyumna), Uttamaujā (is intimate) because he belongs to the same family, Saubhadra (Abhimanyu) (is intimate) because he is the son of Arjuna, The sons of Draupadī (are intimate) because they are the sons of Draupadī; they are indeed intimate allies. Understand that the description, done with an intolerance for the excellence of the enemy, implies that they are not only intimate allies but also Mahārathas. (In the word) "asmākān-tu" (but on our side), the word "tu" (but) serves to exclude the Pāṇḍavas.

ते-तव सम्यग्ज्ञानार्थं तान्ब्रवीमि ।द्विजोत्तमेत्याचार्यसम्बोधनं ॥ ७ ॥त्रि० वि० ॥ अथ स्वेषामन्तरंगत्वाभावं द्योतयितुमाह ॥अस्माकमिति ॥अध्यात्मार्थे ॥हे द्विजोत्तम-द्विजानां सनकादीनां सकाशादुत्तम रुद्र ?अस्माकं मध्ये ये तु-विशिष्टा-बहुश्रेष्ठाः, तु विशब्दस्य बहुनामसु पाठात् ।तान्निबोध-जानी हि ।ये च मम सैन्यस्य नायकाः प्रणेतारः, तान् संज्ञार्थं-नामार्थमुद्दिश्य ब्रवीमि ॥अंशार्थे ।

The address "O best of the twice-born" is a designation for the teacher. (7) *Trivikrama's commentary:* Now, to indicate that they are not intimately connected (to the Supreme), he says: (In the spiritual sense:) O best of the twice-born (Dwijottama) - [meaning] the best, Rudra, from among the twice-born like Sanaka, etc.? But those who are distinguished—very excellent—among us; [the particle] 'tu' [means 'but'] based on its reading in the list of many names. Know them. And those who are the leaders, the guides of my army, them I am naming for the purpose of identification.

हे द्विजोत्तम द्वे शब्दार्थतत्वे जानंति ते द्विजाः कवयः "जाज्ञाने" धातूनामनेकार्थत्वात्, "औणादिकोंत्यलोपोवा", तेषामुत्तम शुक्र "कवीनामुशना कवि" रित्युक्ते ।:तुविशिष्टः-तुना-असुरावेशरूपविशेषेण विशिष्टाः "तुशब्दस्तु विशेषे स्यात्स्वसिद्धान्तेऽवधारणे" इत्युक्तेः,संज्ञार्थ-सम्यग्ज्ञानार्थ ॥प्रसिद्धार्थे हे द्विजोत्तम-ब्राह्मणोत्तम-द्रोण?विशिष्ट शब्दस्य-शिष्टविरोधित्वार्थं मनसि निधाय बहिः श्रेष्ठत्वरूपार्थ दर्शयन्द्वर्थकं तं शब्दं प्रायुंक्त ।भवान्भीष्मश्च कर्णश्च कृपश्च सामतिंजयः ।अश्वत्थामा विकर्णश्च सौमदत्तिस्तथैव च ॥ ८ ॥ये सैन्यस्य नायकाः ममेति संज्ञार्थं तु मदीया इति संज्ञारूपार्थमेव प्राप्ताइति शेषः, नान्ततो मद्धितकारिण इति भावः।तान्ब्रवीमत्यिन्वयः ॥ ७ ॥रा० वि० तानाह द्वाभ्यां ॥ भवानित्यादिना ॥

But they are distinguished—distinguished by a special feature, which is the possession by demons; for the word 'tu' is used to express a particularity or affirmation of one's own conclusion. ये चात्यक्तजीविताः शल्यादयः तेऽपि शूराएव ।अत्यक्तेत्यत्राकारप्रश्लेषाभावे क्तप्रत्ययस्यातीतार्थकत्वेन पूर्वं मृतानामिदानीं योत्धृत्वासम्भवात् । यद्वा जीवितशब्देन तदाशा लक्ष्या ॥ ९ ॥रा० वि० दलद्वये बलिष्ठपरिगणने पांडवविजये मम संदेहोऽस्ति । ततो भवदादयः सर्वे बहुसन्नद्धा भवंत्विति भावेनाह ॥ अपर्याप्तमिति ॥सेनापतिभूतभीष्माभिरक्षितं अस्माकं तत् प्रागुक्तबलिष्ठोपेतं बलं अपर्याप्तं-पांडवजये न समर्थ प्रतीयते। भीमाभिरक्षितमिदं पांडवानां बलं तु पर्याप्तं-अस्मद्विजये शक्तं प्रतीयते ॥ १० ॥नि० वि० प्रशंसारूपसाम्ना सेनापत्यदानेन च भीष्मानुकूल्यं सम्पादितं, इदानीं भेदे-नापि सम्पादयन् तद्धोषजननायाह ॥ अपर्याप्तमिति ॥अध्यात्मार्थे ॥ ॥ अत्र तदित्यव्ययम् तंत्रार्थे तदुभयपक्षमध्यइत्यर्थः । असन्निकृष्टपरामर्शकस्य तस्य सन्निकृष्टपरामर्शकत्वायोगात् । अस्माकं बलं-सैन्यं भीष्माभिरक्षितमिति हेतुगर्भविशेषणम् । देवासुरेषु साम्याद्वर्तमानेन भीष्मेण क्रोधेन तदभिमानिनेति यावत् अभिरक्षितत्वादित्यर्थः । अपर्याप्तं-शत्रूणां जये अलं न भवेत् । यद्वा-परितः-अपर्याप्तं तदस्माकं बलं भीष्माभिरक्षितं । पर्याप्तं त्विदमेतेषां बलं भीमाभिरक्षितं ॥ १० ॥सर्वतः आप्तं-हितकारी नास्ति । अथवा परः-परमात्मा अस्त्यस्मिन्निति परि आप्तं च पर्याप्तं, तद्विरुद्धमपर्याप्तम् विष्णुरहितम्, अनाप्तं चेत्यर्थः । तेषां देवानां तु बलं भीमेन-पूर्णज्ञानिना प्राणेन अभिरक्षितत्वात् । अत्राप्यस्माकमित्यावर्त्यं । दमे-अस्मन्निग्रहे, पर्याप्तं शक्तं । ज्ञानित्वस्येकपक्षएव वर्तमानत्वादिति भावः । अतइति शेषः । इ-आश्चर्यं "इ विचित्रे स्मरे पुमानिति" यादवः । इदमेत-च्छब्दाभ्यां दूरस्थसैन्यतत्पतीनां परामर्शायोगादियमेव योजना युक्ता । परि-विष्णुसहितं आप्तं चेत्ति वा ॥अंशार्थे ॥ भीष्मस्य वस्वंशत्वेन तत्पक्षीयत्वादिति भावः । भीमस्यांशावेशाभ्यामुभाभ्यामप्यस्मत्पक्षानंगीकारादिति च ॥प्रसिद्धार्थ ॥ भीष्मस्योभयपक्षतमानत्वाद्भीमस्यास्मन्निरसने सत्यप्रतिज्ञत्वादिति भावः । वाग्बाणभेदेनापि भीष्मः स्वार्थे यतेतेति ताप्तर्यं ॥ १० ॥
*(Well-known meaning)* The "others" are Jarasandha, Ekalavya, Paundrika, etc., who had abandoned their lives (i.e., were dead). And those like Shalya and others who have *not* abandoned their lives, they too are indeed brave. In the word "atyakta" (not abandoned), because there is no coalescence (or suggestion) of the letter 'ā' (which would change the meaning), and because the suffix '-kta' has a past sense, those who died previously cannot now be possible combatants. Alternatively, by the word "jīvita" (living), that hope (of their being alive/combatants) is indicated. || 9 || In the Rāmānuja Vidyā: "Regarding the counting of the stronger party between the two armies, I have a doubt about the Pandavas' victory. Therefore, may all of you, starting with you (Duryodhana), be well-prepared." With this intent, he says "aparyāptam" (it is insufficient). That army of ours, which is protected by Bhīṣma, who has been made the commander, and which is equipped with the aforementioned strong warriors, is aparyāptam (insufficient/incapable)—it does not seem capable of achieving victory over the Pandavas. However, this army of the Pandavas, protected by Bhīma, is paryāptam (sufficient/capable)—it seems powerful enough to achieve victory over us. || 10 || In the Nīlakaṇṭha Vidyā: Having secured Bhīṣma's favor through conciliation in the form of praise and by giving him the commandership, now, also creating dissension, he (Duryodhana) says "aparyāptam" to generate an outcry from them (the Kauravas). In the Adhyātma interpretation: Here, the word 'tat' is an indeclinable used in the sense of 'there', meaning 'in the midst of both those parties'. This is because that (word 'tat'), which refers to something not nearby, cannot logically refer to something nearby. 'Our army, protected by Bhīṣma' is a description containing the reason. It means that because it is protected by Bhīṣma, who, due to his impartiality between the Devas and Asuras, is filled with anger and is a well-wisher for them (the Pandavas). Aparyāptam means it is not adequate for conquering the enemies. Or, it means 'not attained on all sides'... Insufficient is that our army, protected by Bhīṣma. But sufficient is this army of theirs, protected by Bhīma. || 10 || ...it is not reached/beneficial from all sides. Or, paryāptam means that in which the Supreme Lord (paraḥ) exists, i.e., reached/obtained by Viṣṇu (pari-āptaṃ). Its opposite is aparyāptam, meaning devoid of Viṣṇu, and not obtained. However, the army of those Devas (Pandavas), because it is protected by Bhīma—the full-knower, the (very) life-force (of Viṣṇu)... Here too, 'our' is to be supplied. In dama—in curbing us, it is paryāptam (capable). The idea is that the quality of being a knower exists only on one side. Therefore... 'I' expresses wonder, as in the quote "i is used for wonder and remembrance" from Yādava. Since the words 'idam' (this) and 'etat' (this) cannot logically refer to the army and its leaders who are at a distance, this very interpretation is appropriate. Or, paryāptam means obtained and accompanied by Viṣṇu. In the Aṃśa interpretation: The meaning is that because Bhīṣma, being an aṃśa (portion) of a Vasu, is partial to their side (the Pandavas). And because Bhīma, due to both his being an aṃśa (of Vāyu) and his possession (by that divinity), does not accept our side either. In the well-known meaning (Prasiddhārtha): The idea is that because Bhīṣma is a well-wisher for both sides, and because Bhīma, in rejecting us, is true to his vow. The implied meaning is that even by creating a rift through words (like arrows), Bhīṣma works for his own (true) purpose. || 10 ||

रा० वि० हि यस्मादेवमतोभवंतः सर्व एव सर्वेष्वयनेषु व्यूहप्रवेशमार्गेषु यथाभागमवस्थिताः-स्वस्वरणभूमिस्थानेषु दृढमवास्थताः संतः भीष्ममेवाभिरक्षतु । अन्यैर्युध्यमानो भीष्मः पृष्ठतोऽरिभिर्यथा न हन्येत तथा रक्षन्त्विति द्रोणाचार्यमुक्त्वा विषसाद, ॥११॥त्रि०वि० इदानीं भीष्मोपर्यन्ततः कुपितः सन्बहिर्वर्धयन्सैनिकानाज्ञापयति ॥ अयनेष्विति ॥ ॥ अध्यात्मार्थे ॥ सर्वेष्वयनेषु-जीवस्थानरूपेषु देहेषु । यथाभागं यथाधिकारं, हि-यस्मात्, भीष्मस्य क्रोधस्य रक्षणात्तर्वमस्मदभिप्रेतं भवेदिति भावः ॥॥ अंशार्थे सर्वेष्वयनेषु-भीष्मद्रोणादिसर्ववीरेषु, यथाभागं यथांशं, अवस्थिताः भवन्तः-कालनेम्याद्यः । भीष्मं-क्रोधाभिमानिनं, खरं, कैटभं वा, अभि-रक्षन्तु । तेनैवास्मत्कार्यसिद्धिरिति भावः ॥॥ प्रसिद्धार्थे ॥ ॥ अयनेषु-वाहनेषु, यथाभागं-व्यूहस्य यथोक्तपूर्वादिभाग-अयनेषु च सर्वेषु यथाभागमवस्थिताः । भीष्ममेवाभिरक्षतु भवंतः सर्वएव हि ॥ ११ ॥तस्य संजनयन्हर्ष कुरुवृद्धः पितामहः । सिंहनादं विनद्योच्चैः शंखं दध्मौ प्रतापवान् ॥ १२ ॥मनतिक्रम्यावस्थिताः, सर्वएव भवन्तः भीष्ममेवाभिरक्षन्तु, किमस्मद्रक्षयेत्याक्षेपोक्तिः ॥ वर्धनपक्षे । तस्मिन्राक्षते सति सर्वे वयं रक्षिताःस्थामेति भारः ॥ ११ ॥रा० वि० एवं विषादिनस्तस्य-दुर्योधनस्य हर्ष संजनयन्-जनयितुं "लक्षणहेत्वोः क्रियाया" इति हेतौ शत्रंतं । प्रतापवान्कुरुवृद्धः पितामहो-भीष्मः सिंहनादं उच्चैर्विनद्य शंखं दध्मौ । ध्मा शब्दे ॥ १२ ॥त्रि० वि० सैनिकानां निजरक्षणस्याज्ञापनेन तुष्टः क्रोधाभिमानी, स्वस्वामिनोहर्षजननाय कृतां कृतिमाह ॥ तस्येति ॥॥ अध्यात्मार्थे ॥ ॥ महइत्यत्र पृषोदरादित्वान्मकारोत्तरोकारलोपः । तथा "सप्तसु प्रथमेति" सूत्रात्प्रथमा षष्टचर्थे, तथाच-महः-मोहस्य पिता-उत्पादकः "क्रोधाद्भवति संमोह" इत्युक्तेः ॥कुरुवृद्धः-ककाररेफोत्तरवर्तिनौ याबुकारौ ताभ्यां विभागयोगेन क्रो इति वर्णे स्थिते तेन वृत्-युक्तः धकारो यत्रेति स कुरुवृद्धः-फोधः ।
For, since all of you are thus positioned in all the entrance paths of the military formations according to your respective divisions—firmly stationed in your own battle-ground positions—you must therefore protect Bhīṣma himself. Having said to Droṇācārya, "While others are fighting, protect (him) so that Bhīṣma is not killed from behind by the enemies," he (Duryodhana) sat down, dejected. Now, being angry up to the point of (blaming) Bhīṣma, and mustering the soldiers externally, he commands. In the sense of the higher Self (adhyātma): "In all ayanas" meaning in all bodies which are the abodes of the soul. "According to division" means according to jurisdiction. "For" means "because". The idea is that by protecting the anger of Bhīṣma (i.e., his fiery energy), our objective may be accomplished. In the sense of apportionment (aṃśa): "In all ayanas" means in all heroes like Bhīṣma, Droṇa, etc. "According to division" means according to share. "You all who are positioned" refers to (demons like) Kālanemi, etc. "Protect Bhīṣma" means protect the one who is identified with anger (krodhābhimānin), or (the demons) Khara or Kaiṭabha. The idea is that our purpose is accomplished by this itself. In the well-known sense (prasiddhārtha): "In the ayanas" means in the vehicles (vāhanas). "According to division" means according to the previously mentioned front, etc., parts of the military formation. And (you all), positioned in all the strategic points according to your respective divisions, must indeed protect Bhīṣma himself, all of you, for (this is crucial). The mighty grandfather (pitāmaha), the elder of the Kurus, generating joy in him (Duryodhana), roared a lion's roar loudly and blew his conch. (You all) are positioned without transgressing (your orders). "All of you must protect Bhīṣma himself. Why protect us?" This is an expression of objection. In the interpretation of mustering (vardhana): The implication is that if he (Bhīṣma) is protected, then all of us are protected. Rā. Vi.: Thus, generating joy in him, who was dejected—Duryodhana—in order to produce it, according to the rule "lakṣaṇahetvoḥ kriyāyāḥ" (which governs the use of the affix 'śatṛ' to denote purpose). The mighty (pratāpavān) elder of the Kurus, the grandfather—Bhīṣma—roared a lion's roar loudly and blew his conch. (The root) "dhmā" means 'to produce sound'. Tri. Vi.: The one identified with anger (krodhābhimānī), pleased by the command for the self-protection of the soldiers, describes the act performed to generate joy in his own master. In the sense of the higher Self (adhyātma): In the word "mahaḥ", due to the rule 'pṛṣodarādi' (a grammatical rule governing sandhi), the 'u' sound after the 'm' is elided. Furthermore, according to the sūtra "saptasu prathamā" (a rule from the Sarasvatīkaṇṭhābharaṇa), the nominative case (prathamā) is used in the sense of the genitive (ṣaṣṭhī). And thus, "mahaḥ" means the father—the producer—of delusion (moha), as it is said: "From anger arises delusion."

The one in whom, after the letters 'ka' and 'ra' (which follow), the two letters 'ya' and 'bu' are, through the combination of division, situated in the letter 'kro', and there, a 'dha' letter is joined with a 'vṛt' (circle) – that is the Kuru elder: 'phodha' (a name for Bhīṣma). The demon (asura) who is the presiding deity of that (conch). Producing joy in his army. Having loudly sounded a lion's roar – a mighty laugh – he spoke words, which should be understood. 'Śaṅkha': 'śa' is happiness, 'kha' is that which uproots (khanati) wrong knowledge. He blew – he sounded it, meaning he proclaimed it. And thus, from Viṣṇu, he who has fear, it is said. He who is mighty. 'Pra' is Viṣṇu, according to the statement "a, ka, ya, pra, vi, saṁ, bhū, ma, sa, kha, hā are the designators of Viṣṇu"; therefore, the meaning is 'endowed with the heat (of Viṣṇu)'. त्रि० वि० ॥आनन्तरिकं वृत्तमाह ॥ ततं इति ॥अध्यात्मार्थे ॥शंखः-शमुपलक्षितवस्तुजातकारणं खं-शून्यं येषु ते शंखाः-शून्यवादाः |भेर्यः-भा-भवानी "नामैकदेशग्रहणेन नामग्रहणात्" शक्तिरिति यावत्, ईर्या-प्रतिपाद्या यस्मिन्स भेर्यः-शक्तिवादः ।"पण-व्यवहार" इति धातोः, पणो व्यवहारः, वं बलं यस्मिन्स पणवो-मायावादः ।तस्मिन्व्यावहारिकी यत्तांगीकारात्, ओ-विष्णुः, कः-शब्द-रूपत्वाद्वेदः, तौ कौ यद्वादे न स्तः सोऽनकः-नास्तिकवादः ।गौः-नन्दीश्वरः, यद्वा शाकपार्थिवत्वाद्गोमान्-पशुपतिः मुखः-मुख्यो यस्मिन्सगोमुखः-पाशुपतवादः ।एते सहसैव-बलात्कारादेव अभ्यहन्यन्त "हन हिंसागत्योरिति धातोः" "उपसर्गाणामर्थद्योतकत्वाच्च,, अभ्युपगताः-अंगीकृत्यावर्तिताः इति यावत् ।अनेनैव तेषां निषिद्धत्वं द्योतितम् ।तुमुलः-सान्द्रः ॥
Trivikrama's commentary. He states the subsequent event: "Then..." The spiritual meaning. Śaṅkhas: Those in which 'śa' is the cause of categories of objects denoted by 'śam' (auspiciousness), and 'kha' is void – these are the śaṅkhas: the doctrines of voidism. Bheryas: 'Bhā' is Bhavaānī (Goddess Pārvatī); "by grasping a part of the name, the name is grasped" – meaning Śakti (the Divine Power); 'īryā' is that which is to be proclaimed; in which (doctrine) that exists is bheryaḥ: the doctrine of Śakti. From the root "paṇa" meaning transaction; 'paṇa' is transaction; 'va' is power; in which (doctrine) that exists is paṇavaḥ: the doctrine of illusion (Māyāvāda). Because it accepts a transactional reality in that (world); 'o' is Viṣṇu; 'ka' is the Veda because of its nature as sound and form; in which doctrine those two, 'ka' and 'o' (Veda and Viṣṇu), do not exist, that is ānakaḥ: the atheistic doctrine. 'Gau' is Nandīśvara (Śiva's bull); or, due to being the sovereign of the Śākas (or due to the etymology 'śāka-pārthiva'), 'gomat' is Paśupati (Śiva); 'mukha' is chief; in which (doctrine) that exists is gomukhaḥ: the Pāśupata doctrine. These were suddenly – by force indeed – 'abhyahanyanta' (from the root 'han' meaning to strike or to go); "and because prepositions indicate meaning", they were 'abhyupagatāḥ' – accepted and turned back, meaning. By this very (act), their forbidden nature is indicated.

Tumulaḥ: dense, intense. In the partial meaning and the well-known meaning, the conch (śaṅkha) and the others are simply different types of musical instruments. (13) Rāmānuja Vijaya: Then, after their noise, (he) whose chariot was yoked with white horses – horses of white color; Pāṇḍava – Arjuna. The connection is "remained stationed". Trivikrama's commentary: Now he states the event pertaining to the side of the gods (Pāṇḍavas): "Then, with white..." Spiritual meaning: From the root "ṭu o śvi gativṛdhyoḥ", 'śva' is knowledge; with that, 'ita' is all categories of action characterized by movement; by those which possess this – meaning by the instruments of knowledge and action. 'Hayaiḥ': They go to their objects, thus 'hayaiḥ' are the senses (from the root "haya-gatāv"); 'yukte' – yoked, connected. 'Mahati': In all forms of existence, because it is the best, it is venerable; 'syandane' – in the chariot-shaped human body ("The body is indeed the chariot" - Kāṭhaka). 'Sthitau': Established, sheltered ("Two birds..." Śruti); 'Mādhavaḥ' – the husband of Ramā (Viṣṇu); 'Pāṇḍavaḥ' – 'pāṇḍa' is the object of knowledge, 'vāti' is he who knows, thus that is the jīva (individual soul), meaning. Those two – divine, different from those stated before; the two conches – the doctrines which are the means of knowledge that are the means to happiness; 'pradadhmatuḥ' – they proclaimed. Partial meaning: Mādhava is the Blessed Lord; Pāṇḍava is the man (Arjuna) or Indra. Then, stationed on a great chariot yoked with white horses, Mādhava (Kṛṣṇa) and the Pāṇḍava (Arjuna) blew their divine conches. (14)
