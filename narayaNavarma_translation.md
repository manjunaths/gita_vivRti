ॐ ॥ हरिर्विदध्यान्मम सर्वरक्षां न्यस्तङ्घ्रिपद्मः पतगेन्द्रपृष्ठे । दरारिचर्मासिगदेषुचापपाशान् दधानोऽष्टगुणोऽष्टबाहुः ॥ १२ ॥

May हरि, who has placed his lotus feet upon the back of the king of birds (गरुड), who possesses eight attributes and eight arms bearing the conch, discus, shield, sword, mace, arrow, bow, and noose, bestow all protection upon me.

जलेषु मां रक्षतु मत्स्यमूर्तिर्यादोगणेभ्यो वरुणस्य पाशात् । स्थले च मायावटुवामनोऽव्यात् त्रिविक्रमः खेऽवतु विश्वरूपः ॥ १३ ॥

May the मत्स्य (fish) incarnation protect me in the waters from the hosts of aquatic creatures and the noose of वरुण. On land, may वामन, who appears through his माया, protect me. In the sky, may त्रिविक्रम, the विश्वरूप (universal form), protect me.

दुर्गेष्वटव्याजिमखादिषु प्रभुः पायान्नृसिंहोऽसुरयूथपारिः । विमुञ्चतो यस्य महाट्टहासं दिशो विनेदुर्न्यपतंश्च गर्भाः ॥ १४ ॥

May the Lord नृसिंह, the destroyer of the hosts of demons, protect me in difficulties, forests, battles, and sacrifices. When He roared His great, loud laughter, all directions resounded and the pregnancies (of the demonesses) miscarried.

रक्षत्वसौ माऽध्वनि यज्ञकल्पः स्वदंष्ट्रयोन्नीतधरो वराहः । रामोऽद्रिकूटेष्वथ विप्रवासेसलक्ष्मणोऽव्याद् भरताग्रजो माम् ॥ १५ ॥

May that वराह (boar) incarnation, who is the performer of sacrifice and who lifted the earth on His tusk, protect me on the road. May राम, the elder brother of भरत, along with लक्ष्मण, protect me on mountain peaks and during the exile of the twice-born.

मामुग्रधन्वा अखिलप्रमादान्नारायणः पातु नरश्च हासात् । दत्तस्त्वयोगादथ योगनाथः पायाद् गुणेशः कपिलः कर्मबन्धात् ॥ १६ ॥

May उग्रधन्वा (नारायण) protect me from all heedlessness, and नर from (inappropriate) laughter. May दत्त (दत्तात्रेय) protect me from योग (mischance or inappropriate union), and may योगनाथ (the lord of योग), गुणेश (the lord of qualities), कपिल, protect me from the bondage of karma.

सनत्कुमारोऽवतु कामदेवाद्धयशीर्षो मां पथि देवहेळनात् । देवर्षिवर्यः पुरुषान्तरार्चनात् कूर्मो हरिर्मां निरयादशेषात् ॥ १७ ॥

May सनत्कुमार protect me from कामदेव (the god of love). May हयशीर्ष (the horse-headed form) protect me on the path from offence to the gods. May the foremost of divine sages (नारद) protect me from worshipping another soul (or deity) as the Supreme Person. May हरि in the form of कूर्म (the tortoise) protect me from all hells.

धन्वन्तरिर्भगवान् पात्वपथ्याद् द्वन्द्वाद् भयाद् ऋषभो निर्जितात्मा । यज्ञश्च लोकादथ तत्कृतान्नो बलो गणात् क्रोधवशादहीन्द्रः ॥ १८ ॥

May भगवान् धन्वन्तरि protect me from what is unwholesome. May ऋषभ, the self-conquered one, protect me from the fear of duality. May यज्ञ (the Lord as यज्ञ) protect me from the worldly people, and बल (बलराम) from the food offered to them. May अहीन्द्र (the lord of serpents, शेष) protect me from the subjugation of anger.

द्वैपायनो भगवान् सम्प्रमोहात् बुद्धस्तु पाषण्डिगणात् प्रमादात् । कल्की कलेः कालमलात् प्रपातु धर्मावनायोरुकृतावतारः ॥ १९ ॥

May भगवान् द्वैपायन (वेदव्यास) protect me from delusion. May बुद्ध protect me from the hosts of heretics and from carelessness. May कल्कि, who has taken a great incarnation for the protection of righteousness, protect me from the filth of the कलि age.

मां केशवो गदया प्रातरव्याद् गोविन्द आसङ्गव आत्तवेणुः । नारायणः पातु सदाऽऽत्तशक्तिर्मध्यन्दिने विष्णुररीन्द्रपाणिः ॥ २० ॥

May केशव protect me in the morning with His mace. May गोविन्द, who has taken up His flute, protect me at the time of milking (the latter part of the day). May नारायण, who is always possessed of divine potency, protect me. May विष्णु, with the discus (the enemy of lords of enemies) in His hand, protect me at midday.

देवोऽपराह्णे मधुहोग्रधन्वा सायं त्रिधामाऽवतु माधवो माम् । दोषे हृषीकेश उतार्धरात्रे निशीथ एकोऽवतु पद्मनाभः ॥ २१ ॥

May the God मधुहा (the slayer of मधु), उग्रधन्वा, protect me in the afternoon. May त्रिधामा माधव protect me in the evening. May हृषीकेश protect me at dusk (first part of the night). And may पद्मनाभ, the one without a second, protect me at midnight.

श्रीवत्सलक्ष्माऽपररात्र ईशः प्रत्यूष ईशोऽसिधरो जनार्दनः । दामोदरोऽव्यादनुसन्ध्यं प्रभाते विष्णुः श्रीमान् भगवान् कालमूर्तिः ॥ २२ ॥

May ईश, who bears the श्रीवत्स mark, protect me in the last part of the night. May जनार्दन, the wielder of the sword, protect me at dawn. May दामोदर protect me at every twilight. May भगवान् विष्णु, the glorious one, who is the form of time, protect me at early dawn (प्रभात).

चक्रं युगान्तानलतिग्मनेमि भ्रमत् समान्ताद् भगवत्प्रयुक्तम् । दन्दग्धि दन्दग्ध्यरिसैन्यमाशु कक्षं यथा वातसखो हुताशः ॥ २३ ॥

O discus, whose fierce rim is like the fire at the end of an age, revolving and hurled by the Lord, burn, burn quickly the army of enemies, just as fire, aided by the wind, burns a thicket.

गदेऽशनिस्पर्शनविष्फुलिङ्गे निष्पिण्ढि निष्पिण्ढ्यजितप्रियाऽसि । कूष्माण्डवैनायकयक्षरक्षोभूतग्रहांश्चूर्णय चूर्णयारीन् ॥ २४ ॥

O mace, whose sparks have the touch of thunderbolts, you are the beloved of the unconquerable Lord (अजित). Pound, pound (the demons). Crush, crush the enemies—the कूष्माण्ड, वैनायक, यक्ष, राक्षस, भूत, and ग्रह demons.

त्वं यातुधानप्रमथप्रेतमातृपिशाचविप्रग्रहघोरदृष्टीन् । दरेन्द्र विद्रावय कृष्णपूरितो भीमस्वनोऽरीन् हृदयानि कम्पयन् ॥ २५ ॥

O best of conches (दरेन्द्र), filled by कृष्ण, making a fearsome sound and causing the hearts of enemies to tremble, you drive away the यातुधान, प्रमथ, प्रेत, मातृका, पिशाच, विप्रग्रह, and those with terrible looks.

त्वं तिग्मधारासिवरारिसैन्यमीशप्रयुक्तो मम छिन्धि छिन्धि । चक्षूंषि चर्मञ्छतचन्द्र छादय द्विषामघं नो हर पापचक्षुषाम् ॥ २६ ॥

O supreme sword with sharp edges, hurled by the Lord, cut, cut apart the army of my enemies. O shield with a hundred moon-like spots, cover the eyes of the malevolent. O Lord, remove the sin of those with sinful vision (who look upon us with evil intent).

यन्नो भयं ग्रहेभ्योऽभूत् केतुभ्यो नृभ्य एव च । सरीसृपेभ्यो दंष्ट्रिभ्यो भूतेभ्योऽघेभ्य एव च ॥ २७ ॥

Whatever fear has arisen for us from the planets, from comets, from men, from reptiles, from those with fangs, from ghosts, and from sinners—

सर्वाण्येतानि भगवन्नामरूपास्त्रकीर्तनात् । प्रयान्तु सङ्क्षयं सद्यो येऽन्ये श्रेयः प्रतीपकाः ॥ २८ ॥

May all these, and whatever other things are obstacles to our welfare, by the chanting of the Lord's names, forms, and weapons, quickly attain destruction.

गरुडो भगवान् स्तोत्रस्तोमच्छन्दोमयः प्रभुः । रक्षत्वशेषकृच्छ्रेभ्यो विष्वक्सेनः स्वनामभिः ॥ २९ ॥

May भगवान् गरुड, who is the embodiment of the hymns, verses, and metres (of this prayer), protect us. May विष्वक्सेन, the commander, protect us from all kinds of afflictions by means of His own names.

सर्वापद्भयो हरेर्नामरूपयानायुधानि नः । बुद्धीन्द्रियमनःप्राणान् पान्तु पार्षदभूषणाः ॥ ३० ॥

May the names, forms, vehicles, and weapons of हरि, which are the ornaments of His associates, protect our intellect, senses, mind, and life-breaths from all dangers and calamities.

यथा हि भगवानेव वस्तुतः सदसच्च यत् । सत्येनानेन नः सर्वे यान्तु नाशमुपद्रवाः ॥ ३१ ॥

Just as भगवान indeed is, in reality, all that is existent and non-existent (the cause and effect), by this truth, may all our afflictions come to naught.

यथैकात्म्यानुभावेन विकल्परहितः स्वयम् । भूषणायुधलिङ्गाख्या धत्ते शक्तीः स्वमायया ॥ ३२ ॥

Just as by virtue of His perception of oneness (with His own forms), He Himself, being free from distinctions, assumes, through His own माया, the potencies in the form of ornaments, weapons, and symbols (like the conch, discus, etc.).

तेनैव सत्यमानेन सर्वज्ञो भगवान् हरिः । पातु सर्वैः स्वरूपैर्नः सदा सर्वत्र सर्वगः ॥ ३३ ॥

By that same principle of truth, may the omniscient भगवान् हरि, who is present everywhere, protect us always, everywhere, with all His forms.

विदिक्षु दिक्षुर्ध्वमधः समन्तादन्तर्बहिर्भगवान् नारसिंहः । प्रहापयँल्लोकभयं स्वनेन स्वतेजसा ग्रस्तसमस्ततेजाः ॥ ३४ ॥

May भगवान् नारसिंह, who has swallowed up all (others') tejas with His own tejas, who dispels the fear of the worlds with His roar, protect us in the corners, in the cardinal directions, above, below, everywhere, within, and without.

मघवन्निदमाख्यातं वर्म नारायणात्मकम् । विजेष्यस्यञ्जसा येन दंसितोऽसुरयूथपान् ॥ ३५ ॥

O मघवन् (इन्द्र), this armour, which is comprised of नारायण, has been described to you. Armed with this, you will surely and easily conquer the leaders of the demon hordes.

एतद् धारयमाणस्तु यं यं पश्यति चक्षुषा । पदा वा संस्पृशेत् सद्यः साध्वसात् स विमुच्यते ॥ ३६ ॥

Whoever, while bearing this (armour), sees any being with his eye, or touches one with his foot, that being is immediately freed from fear.

न कुतश्चिद् भयं तस्य विद्यां धारयतो भवेत् । राजदस्युग्रहादिभ्यो व्याध्यादिभ्यश्च कर्हिचित् ॥ ३७ ॥

For one who bears this prayer (vidyā), there is no fear from anywhere whatsoever—neither from kings, thieves, demons, and the like, nor from diseases and the like, at any time.

इमां विद्यां पुरा कश्चित् कौशिको धारयन् द्विजः । योगधारणया स्वाङ्गं जहौ स मरुधन्वनि ॥ ३८ ॥

Once upon a time, a certain ब्राह्मण named कौशिक, while bearing this prayer (vidyā), abandoned his body through yogic concentration in the desert of मरुधन्व.

तस्योपरि विमानेन गन्धर्वपतिरेकदा । ययौ चित्ररथः स्त्रीभिर्वृतो यत्र द्विजक्षयः ॥ ३९ ॥

Once, चित्ररथ, the king of the गन्धर्वs, surrounded by women, was passing over that very spot where the ब्राह्मण's remains lay, in his celestial chariot.

साङ्गनो न्यपतत् सद्यः सविमानो ह्यवाक्शिराः । विद्यामिमां धारयतो मृतस्यास्थिविलङ्घनात् ॥ ४० ॥

Along with his women and his chariot, he instantly fell headlong, because he had crossed over the bones of the deceased one who had borne this prayer (vidyā).

स बालखिल्यवचनादस्थीन्यादाय' विस्मितः । प्रास्य प्राचीसरस्वत्यां स्नात्वा धाम स्वमन्वगात् ॥ ४१ ॥

Then, amazed, at the words of the बालखिल्य sages, he gathered up the bones, cast them into the eastern सरस्वती river, bathed, and regained his own abode.

य इदं शृणुयात् काले यो धारयति चादृतः । तं नमस्यन्ति भूतानि मुच्यते सर्वतो भयात् ।। ४२ ।।

Whoever listens to this at the proper time, and whoever respectfully bears it (wears or recites it), all beings bow down to him, and he is freed from all fear.

एतां विद्यामधिगतो विश्वरूपाच्छतक्रतुः । त्रैलोक्यलक्ष्मीं बुभुजे विनिर्जित्य मृधेऽसुरान् ॥ ४३ ॥

Having obtained this prayer (vidyā) from विश्वरूप, शतक्रतु (इन्दra) enjoyed the splendour of the three worlds, after completely defeating the demons in battle.
